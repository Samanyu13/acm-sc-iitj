iS3: iDeathon on Sustainable Smart Systems

iS3 is the science and technology idea generation event organised by ACM Student Chapter IIT Jodhpur in association with AVL India. It provides a platform for the IIT Jodhpur students to showcase their technical prowess for a sustainable environment. iS3 challenge is the platform for young brains to showcase their ideas and compete with their peers. It is a good opportunity for the IITJ students to rack their brains to provide solutions to different kinds of sectors for removing traditional and digital waste from their processes, organization, products, and services, to radically improve the experience of their ecosystems. The Theme for the event is Sustainable Smart Systems meaning that the participants should frame their Proof of concept (Working Model) which is relevant to Industrial Cost Cutting, Digitization of factories, Quality Control, Energy Saving, Safety & Security, etc. The Concept of IoT and Lean Digital Transformation has the capability to develop smart sustainable systems for the future.

About

Objective
iDeathon is a fast-paced, intensive brainstorming session that helps people to come up with creative digital transformation solutions to ongoing issues in different sectors. Participants need to explore potential ideas in groups using cutting-edge ideation techniques, including design thinking.

Following are the key areas where participants need to focus and need to develop a working model (PoC) -
  • Smart Home Applications
  • Health Care & Hospitality
  • Smart Cities
  • Agriculture
  • Factory Digitalization
  • Product flow Monitoring
  • Inventory Management
  • Public Safety and Security
  • Quality Control
  • Packaging optimization
  • Logistics and Supply Chain Optimization
  • Energy Saving/Energy Management
  • Smart Grid
  • Buildings & construction

Note: The topics are not just limited to these areas, participants are open to framing their idea to solve any real-life problem.
Expected Outcomes
Participants must have to find out real-time problems relevant to digital transformation from different sectors, and need to develop the solution for those real-time problems with proof of concept (Working Model).
Proof of concept (Working Model) must be relevant to Lean Digital Transformation, Industrial Cost Cutting, Digitization of factories, Energy Saving, Process Digitization, etc. by applying the Internet of Things Concept.

Guidelines for Proof of Concept (PoC) submission - Detailed Requirements

Level 1 - Guidelines

Problem Statement - "Digital Transformation using Internet of Things"

The benefits of the Internet of Things include improved productivity and efficiency, better flexibility and agility, and increased profitability. IoT also improves the customer experience.

While Smart Factory technologies are interesting and exciting, the benefits of the Internet of Things should always be central to any discussion. After all, any investment you make in technology, improved manufacturing processes, or enhanced systems should deliver a return.

This is a highly competitive space, and the Internet of Things, combined with automation and machine learning, is helping organizations stay competitive by reducing perational costs and increasing productivity/efficiency.

You need to come up with a Proof of Concept (Working Model) using the Internet of Things which could be in the form of Software Coding or Software + Hardware or a Web-based solution to tackle the problem of Industrial Sectors like Manufacturing, Production, Maintenance, Power system, Safety, Energy Management, Energy Saving, Quality Control, Security, Healthcare, Agriculture, Buildings, Construction, Logistics, Supply chain, Packaging, Inventory Management etc.

Participant's need to be focused on Industrial Internet of Things (IIOT) based Digital Transformation, Lean Manufacturing, Cost Optimization, Efficiency Improvement, Automated Workflow, condition-based monitoring, Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning models for any kind of business / Factory / Workshop / Retail / Service sector. This should be in the domain of low-cost automation, Industrial Internet of Things, Industry 4.0, Data Analytics, Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning models.

The proposed Solution (PoC) should have the following format:-

  • Title of the proposed PoC: A heading that best describes your solution.
  • Problem Statement of PoC: Identify the particular aspect of the Internet of Thing's your solution is for.
  • The proposed PoC of the problem: Propose a solution and explain how your solution is going to solve the problem of industries using your IOT concept.
  • Expected impact and conclusion: Justify why you think your idea will be a commercial business success in terms of the impact that it will create. There should be a clear definition of how the impact will be measured. Define Measurement matrix.

Your Solution (PoC) will be judged on the basis of:-

  • Feasibility: Whether your idea is practical and can be implemented
  • Impact: Whether your idea can have a real impact or bring about a change in society/industries.
  • Scalability: Whether your idea could be scaled to a larger level.
  • Originality: Whether your idea is self-inspired and innovative and not copied from somewhere.

This should be in the domain of low-cost automation, Industrial Internet of Things, Industry 4.0, Data Analytics, Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning models.

Format for Proof of concept (PoC)

The PoC reports should be like Working Model: concise and focusing on what you did.

Follow the instructions below -
  • Introduction
    • Why is your topic important ? (convince us)
    • What you will talk/do about ?
    • Where is it used ? Applications
  • Background
    • Any relevant and specific info e.g. software/hardware statistics, equipment used.
    • What other people had to say on this topic.
    • What other people did on this topic (or related topics).
    • Problems and shortcomings of their work
    • How your work is different and better
  • Proof of Concept
    • Your approach to the problem
    • What you did
    • Design
    • What did/didn't work?
    • Include Coding, Programs, Graphs, Equations, Pictures, etc. as appropriate.
    • Results: Include relevant observations, measurements, and statistics. For example, Include statistics such as timing information if available by simulation, or if not, your own analysis about the critical path, delays, and clock cycles.
  • Conclusion
    • What was accomplished/learned
    • What you would have done differently
    • Future work
  • References
    • Cite the papers/books that you used
    • Anything you found useful
    • Include articles from digital magazines if you used them.
  • Things to include in the PoC report
    • Coding, Programming, Equations, Graphs, Figures
    • Pictures, Your observations and measurements
    • Simulation, model (E.g. our design took 200 cycles to do task #1. We ran it 10 times, and the time for each run was 12, 15, 16, 14, 13, 15. Therefore, we expect the second task, which is twice as long, to take about 30 sec).

Note: All reports should be in soft pdf form.
